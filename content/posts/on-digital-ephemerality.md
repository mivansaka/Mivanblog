We live in an age of unprecedented digital production, yet we're creating the most ephemeral artifacts in human history. Our tweets disappear into feeds, our stories vanish after 24 hours, our thoughts are scattered across platforms that may not exist in a decade.

## The Paradox of Abundance

Never before has it been so easy to create and publish. Never before has so much been created and so little been preserved.

The digital libraries of Alexandria are burning, and we're too busy posting to notice.

## Personal Archiving

I've started treating my digital output with the care of an archivist:

1. **Own your domain** — URLs should never break
2. **Plain text formats** — Markdown over proprietary formats
3. **Version control** — Git for everything important
4. **Multiple backups** — Local, cloud, and physical
5. **Regular exports** — From social platforms

## Building for Permanence

What we build online should outlast us. Not because of ego, but because our digital artifacts are the primary sources of our era.

Future historians will judge us by what we preserved, not what we produced.
