In an age of constant notification and infinite scroll, silence has become a luxury good. Yet the architecture of our digital spaces doesn't have to demand our attention at every turn.

## The Attention Economy

Modern software design is built on the premise that attention is finite and valuable. Every interface element competes for your focus, your thumb, your next click. This is the architecture of noise.

## Designing for Quiet

What would it mean to design digital spaces that respect silence? That don't demand constant engagement?

- **No notifications by default**
- **No infinite scroll**
- **No red badges screaming for attention**
- **No auto-playing content**

The architecture of silence is not about removing features. It's about designing spaces that breathe.

## A Return to Intention

When we design for quiet, we create spaces for intention. Spaces where users choose to engage, rather than being compelled to.

This is the future I want to build.
